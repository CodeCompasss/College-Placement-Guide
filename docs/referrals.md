# Referral Request Guide

Getting a referral increases your chances of landing interviews because it bypasses early resume screening.

## 🎯 Goal
To politely and effectively ask someone inside a company to refer you for a job role.

---

## 🧠 Before You Ask for a Referral

Make sure you:
- Have a tailored resume for the role
- Know the exact job title + link
- Understand the company basics
- Have a relevant LinkedIn profile

---

## 📌 How to Find People to Ask
- LinkedIn employees at target company
- Alumni from your college
- Friends/relatives working in tech
- GitHub contributors / open source maintainers

---

## 💬 Referral Request Message (Template)

### Short LinkedIn Message

Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well. I’m applying for the [Job Title] role at [Company Name]. I found your profile and noticed you work there.

I’m very interested in this opportunity because of my experience in [your skill/field].

Would you be comfortable referring me for this role? I can share my resume and job details.

Thank you so much for your time!

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## 📎 If they agree, send:

- Resume PDF
- Job link
- 2–3 line summary of your skills
- Why you're a fit

---

## ❌ Common Mistakes
- Asking without context
- Sending long paragraphs
- Not specifying job role
- Not customizing message

---

## ✅ Pro Tip
Always personalize at least 1 line (mention their role or company project).# LinkedIn Networking for Referrals

LinkedIn is one of the most powerful tools to get referrals if used correctly.

---

## 🎯 Goal
Build real connections before asking for a referral.

---

## 🔍 Step 1: Find the Right People

Search:
- "Software Engineer at [Company]"
- "Recruiter at [Company]"
- "Hiring Manager [Team]"

Filter by:
- 1st degree connections (best)
- Alumni from your college
- People with shared interests

---

## 🤝 Step 2: Send Connection Request

### Simple Connection Note

Hi [Name],  
I’m exploring opportunities in [domain] and would love to connect with you.

---

## 💬 Step 3: Start Conversation (DON’T ask referral immediately)

Hi [Name],

Thanks for connecting! I’m currently exploring roles in [field]. I really admire your work at [Company].

How has your experience been there?

---

## 🎯 Step 4: Ask for Referral (after reply)

Hi [Name],

Thanks for sharing that!

I’m actually applying for the [Job Title] role at [Company]. If you feel comfortable, would you be open to referring me?

I can share my resume and details.

---

## 🚀 Engagement Tips
- Like/comment on their posts before messaging
- Keep messages under 100–120 words
- Don’t spam multiple follow-ups

---

## ❌ Avoid
- “Can you refer me?” as first message
- Copy-paste mass messages
- Being pushy

---

## ✅ Pro Tip
Warm connections convert 3–5x better than cold referral requests.
# Recruiter Outreach for Referrals & Job Opportunities

Recruiters can directly move your profile to interview stages if you approach them correctly.

---

## 🎯 Goal
Get noticed by recruiters and increase chances of interview calls or referrals internally.

---

## 🔍 Step 1: Find Recruiters

Search on LinkedIn:
- "Technical Recruiter at [Company]"
- "Talent Acquisition [Company]"
- "HR Business Partner [Company]"

---

## 📩 Step 2: Cold Outreach Message

### Short Recruiter Message

Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well. I’m currently exploring opportunities in [domain/role].

I’ve applied for the [Job Title] position at [Company Name] and wanted to reach out directly.

My background includes:
- [Skill 1]
- [Skill 2]
- [Key project or achievement]

If the role is still open, I would really appreciate it if you could review my profile or guide me on next steps.

Resume attached for reference.

Thank you for your time!

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## 📎 Step 3: Follow-Up Message (after 5–7 days)

Hi [Name],

Just following up on my previous message regarding the [Job Title] role at [Company Name].

I remain very interested in the opportunity and would appreciate any update or feedback when convenient.

Thank you!

---

## 🧠 Tips to Increase Response Rate

- Keep message under 150–200 words
- Mention job ID if available
- Attach resume only once
- Be polite, not desperate

---

## ❌ Mistakes to Avoid

- Spamming multiple recruiters
- Sending generic messages
- Asking “Any updates?” repeatedly
- Not including role details

---

## 🚀 Pro Tip
Recruiters respond more when you clearly show:
- Fit for role
- Concise achievements
- Clear intent (not vague job search)
