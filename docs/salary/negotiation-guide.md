# Salary Negotiation Guide

Salary negotiation is not about asking aggressively — it’s about clearly communicating your value and market alignment.

---

## 🎯 Goal
Maximize your offer by negotiating base salary, bonus, or benefits after receiving an offer.

---

## ⏰ When to Negotiate
- AFTER receiving an offer (not before)
- BEFORE signing the contract
- Within 24–72 hours ideally

---

## 🧠 Key Principles
- Never accept first offer immediately
- Be polite, not demanding
- Justify with market data or competing offers
- Focus on total compensation, not just salary

---

## 💬 Negotiation Email Template

Hi [Hiring Manager Name],

Thank you so much for the offer for the [Role Title] position. I’m excited about the opportunity to join [Company Name].

After reviewing the offer, I was hoping to discuss the base salary component. Based on my research and market benchmarks for similar roles, I was expecting something closer to [Your Target Range].

Is there any flexibility in the compensation package?

I’m very excited about the role and confident I can contribute strongly to the team.

Looking forward to your thoughts.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## 📊 What You Can Negotiate
- Base salary
- Joining bonus
- Performance bonus
- Stock/RSUs
- Remote flexibility
- Signing date

---

## ❌ Mistakes to Avoid
- Accepting too quickly
- Making emotional arguments
- Bluffing fake offers
- Being aggressive or rude

---

## 🚀 Pro Tip
Always anchor with a range, not a single number.
