# 💰 Salary Negotiation Guide

Salary negotiation is not confrontation.

It is **structured value communication + market alignment**.

> Companies expect negotiation.
> Silence often leaves money on the table.

---

# 🎯 Goal

Maximize total compensation by negotiating:

* Base salary
* Bonus
* Equity (RSUs / ESOPs)
* Benefits

---

# ⏰ When to Negotiate (Critical Timing)

Only negotiate:

* AFTER receiving an offer
* BEFORE signing the contract
* Within 24–72 hours

# 🧠 Core Negotiation Principles

---

## 1. ⚖️ Never Accept First Offer Immediately

First offer = starting point, not final offer.

---

## 2. 🧩 Be Data-Driven, Not Emotional

Use:

* market salary data
* peer benchmarks
* competing offers

📘 Salary benchmarking:

* [Glassdoor Salary Insights](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/index.htm?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 3. 🎯 Focus on Total Compensation

Not just base salary.

Include:

* stock value
* bonus structure
* benefits

---

## 4. 🧠 Use Anchoring Strategy

Always give:

* salary range (not single number)

Example:

> “I was expecting something in the range of X–Y based on market standards.”

---

# 💬 Salary Negotiation Email Template

---

Hi [Hiring Manager Name],

Thank you for the offer for the [Role] position at [Company]. I truly appreciate the opportunity and I’m excited about the role.

After reviewing the offer, I wanted to discuss the base compensation. Based on my research and current market benchmarks for similar roles, I was expecting a range closer to [X–Y].

Is there any flexibility in the compensation structure?

I am very enthusiastic about contributing to the team and confident I can add strong value.

Looking forward to your response.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

# ❌ Common Mistakes

* Accepting immediately without evaluation
* Being emotional or defensive
* Using fake competing offers
* Demanding instead of discussing
* Negotiating before offer is received

---

# 🚀 Pro-Level Strategy

### 1. Timing Advantage

Wait until they are invested in you.

### 2. Silent Leverage

Your interview performance = your strongest bargaining power.

### 3. Anchoring Range

Never say:

* “I want 12 LPA”

Say:

* “I am looking in the range of 10–14 LPA”
