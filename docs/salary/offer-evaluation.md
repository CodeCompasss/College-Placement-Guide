# 📄 Offer Evaluation Guide

Not all high-paying offers are good offers.

A high salary can still be a **low-value career decision**.

> You are not evaluating money.
> You are evaluating **total career value over time**.

---

# 🎯 Goal

Decide whether to:

* Accept
* Reject
* Negotiate

based on **total compensation + growth potential**

---

# 📦 Key Components of an Offer

Every offer is a mix of:

* Base Salary
* Bonus (performance / annual)
* Stocks (RSUs / ESOPs)
* Joining Bonus
* Benefits (insurance, PTO, perks)

📘 Compensation structure reference:

* [Investopedia Compensation Guide](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/c/compensation.asp?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# 🧠 Offer Evaluation Framework

---

## 1. 💰 Base Salary (Stability Layer)

This is your guaranteed income.

* Monthly fixed pay
* Financial safety
* Loan eligibility impact

> This is the foundation of your offer.

---

## 2. 📊 Variable Pay (Uncertainty Layer)

Includes:

* performance bonus
* target-based incentives

⚠️ Key insight:

> High variable pay = high uncertainty

📘 Salary structure breakdown:

* [Glassdoor Salary Insights](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/index.htm?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 3. 📈 Equity (Long-Term Wealth Layer)

Stocks (RSUs / ESOPs):

* High risk
* High long-term reward
* Depends on company growth

### Critical factor:

* Vesting schedule (usually 4 years)

📘 Equity explanation:

* [SEC Equity Compensation Guide](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/investing-basics/glossary/equity-compensation?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 4. 🧘 Work-Life Balance (Sustainability Layer)

Evaluate:

* working hours
* weekend culture
* remote flexibility
* burnout risk

📘 Work culture research:

* [Harvard Work Culture Studies](https://hbr.org/topic/work-life-balance?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 5. 📈 Career Growth (Future Value Layer)

Check:

* learning curve
* tech stack relevance
* mentorship
* brand value

📘 Career growth insight:

* [LinkedIn Career Growth Research](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/topics/career-advice?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# 📊 Simple Scoring System

Rate each out of 10:

* Salary Stability
* Growth Opportunity
* Work-Life Balance
* Brand Value

### Decision Rule:

* 32–40 → Strong offer
* 24–31 → Average offer
* <24 → Weak offer

---

# ❌ Red Flags (Do NOT Ignore)

* Unclear stock valuation
* Unrealistic bonus structure
* “Unlimited work” culture
* No clear role definition
* No career progression path

---

# 🚀 Pro Insight

> A lower-paying offer at a top company often outperforms a higher-paying offer at a stagnant company.

Reason:

* faster learning
* better network
* stronger resume value
* higher future salary jumps
