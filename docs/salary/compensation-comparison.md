# 📊 Compensation Comparison Guide

Comparing job offers is not about salary.

It is about **total economic + career value over time**.

> The highest base salary is often NOT the best offer.

---

# 🎯 Goal

Convert multiple job offers into a **single comparable value system**:

> “True Annual + Risk-adjusted + Growth-adjusted Compensation”

---

# 🧮 Step 1: Normalize Total Compensation (TC)

Convert everything into annual value:

* Base Salary (guaranteed)
* Bonus (expected value, not maximum)
* Stocks (vested yearly value)
* Benefits (monetized estimate)

📘 Compensation structure reference:

* [Investopedia Total Compensation Guide](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/c/compensation.asp?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# 📊 Step 2: Standard Offer Breakdown Model

---

## 🟢 Offer A

* Base: $80,000
* Bonus: 10% = $8,000
* Stock: $12,000/year

### Total Value:

> $100,000/year

---

## 🔵 Offer B

* Base: $90,000
* Bonus: 5% = $4,500
* Stock: $0

### Total Value:

> $94,500/year

---

### 🧠 Insight:

> Offer A > Offer B despite lower base salary

---

# 📉 Step 3: Risk Adjustment (VERY IMPORTANT)

Not all money is equal.

Apply discount for risk:

* Private company stocks → high uncertainty
* Performance bonuses → variable payout
* Early-stage startups → liquidity risk

---

### Risk-adjusted thinking:

> Expected Value = Probability × Payout

📘 Financial reasoning model:

* [Harvard Business Risk Analysis](https://hbr.org/topic/risk-management?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# 📈 Step 4: Add Non-Cash Benefits

Convert perks into monetary equivalents:

* Remote work → +$3k–$10k value
* Health insurance → medical cost savings
* Paid leave → time value
* Learning budget → skill investment value

---

📘 Work value research:

* [OECD Work Benefits Study](https://www.oecd.org/employment/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

# 🧠 Decision Matrix (Weighted Model)

| Factor            | Weight |
| ----------------- | ------ |
| Base Salary       | 40%    |
| Growth Potential  | 25%    |
| Work-Life Balance | 20%    |
| Benefits          | 15%    |

---

### Final Score Formula:

> Total Score = Weighted Sum of all factors

---

# ❌ Common Mistakes

* Only comparing base salary
* Ignoring stock vesting schedules
* Not adjusting for taxes
* Comparing different job levels blindly
* Ignoring long-term growth
