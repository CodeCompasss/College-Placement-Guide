# Compensation Comparison Guide

Comparing job offers requires converting everything into a single "total value" view.

---

## 🎯 Goal
Compare multiple offers fairly using a standardized framework.

---

## 📊 Step 1: Normalize Salary

Convert everything to annual compensation:

- Base Salary (annual)
- Bonus (expected %)
- Stocks (vested yearly value)
- Benefits (approx value)

---

## 🧮 Step 2: Example Calculation

### Offer A
- Base: $80,000
- Bonus: 10% = $8,000
- Stock: $12,000/year  
**Total = $100,000**

---

### Offer B
- Base: $90,000
- Bonus: 5% = $4,500
- Stock: $0  
**Total = $94,500**

👉 Offer A is better overall despite lower base salary

---

## 📉 Step 3: Adjust for Risk

Reduce value if:
- Stocks are private/unlisted (risk factor)
- Bonus is performance-heavy
- Company stability is uncertain

---

## 📈 Step 4: Adjust for Benefits

Add value for:
- Remote flexibility (+$3k–$10k equivalent)
- Health insurance
- Paid leave
- Learning budget

---

## 🧠 Decision Matrix

| Factor            | Weight |
|------------------|--------|
| Base Salary       | 40%    |
| Growth Potential  | 25%    |
| Work-Life Balance | 20%    |
| Benefits          | 15%    |

---

## ❌ Mistakes to Avoid
- Judging only base salary
- Ignoring stock vesting schedule
- Not considering taxes
- Comparing different job levels blindly

---

## 🚀 Pro Tip
Always compare **"real money you can take home over 2–4 years"**, not monthly salary alone.
